description: 'This pathway provides an overview of TGFB1 signalling on dNK cells with
  downstream effects of phenotypic conversion, suppressed cytotoxicity, and stimulation
  of processes such as trophoblast invasion and spiral artery remodeling as a result
  of the signalling. Within the dNK cells an inhibitory feedback loop of the TGF-B
  signalling is also occurring as a result of the protein SMAD7 binding to TGFBR1
  preventing its phosphorylation, and SMURF2 which catalyses the ubiquitination of
  TGFBR1. Following activation of TGFBR2, TGFBR1 is phosphorylated and triggers the
  phosphorylation of SMAD2 and SMAD3 which bind to SMAD4 forming a complex following
  the phosphorylation. The SMAD2/3:4 complex then translocates to the nucleus where
  work is done to suppress the activation of proteins FCGR3A (aka CD16), IFNG, and
  NKG2D which usually give NK cells their cytotoxic phenotype.  The complex also works
  to activate transcription of genes translating CD103, CD49A, KIR2DL4, and CD9 which
  work to promote a tolerogenic phenotypic conversion/differentiation of NK cells
  within the decidua from CD16+ to CD16-. The suppressed cytotoxicity triggered by
  the complex allows for the activation of cytokines  such as IL8, CXCL10 which stimulate
  trophoblast invasion and factors such as VEGFC, PLGF, ANGPT1, and ANGPT2 which stimulate
  the spiral artery remodelling. '
